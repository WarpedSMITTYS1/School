Monsanto Has Been Sued!

Monsanto, a major player in the agrochemical and agricultural biochemical industry, has recently been sued by thousands of farmers like you and me. These farmers have finally put an end to the reign of terror ran by Monsanto and their co-conspirators over the past decade.
by Jacob Smith - August 22, 2018
OVER THE YEARS, Monsanto has run a global monopoly of the farming industry surrounding bio-engineered seeds and herbicides and fixed prices. In 2000, they were sued in the US District Court for the District of Columbia (D.C).
The lawsuit, filed by the The Foundation of Economic Trends and the National Family Farm Coalition, served as a voice for domestic and international farmers who purchased Monsanto’s Genetically Modified (GM) crops like corn and soybeans.
An uncovered document dating from 1996 is understood as a confirmation of the global monopoly Monsanto and their co-conspirators (Like Dupont, Dow, etc.) held. The document titled “Maize Protection Business Plan” includes the idea of the monopoly on and the restraint on GM crops. It also states how their monopoly made sure no other GM producers would ever make it to the market.
On top of the alleged monopoly, the lawsuit also claims that Monsanto never did the proper research into their GM seeds. Tests targeting human health and safety were alleged to be incomplete before the seeds were marketed. Therefore, the general trust in GM foods lowered drastically, and the US economy, as well as numerous foreign economies, took a pretty sizable hit.
Even those farmers who didn’t purchase or grow their GM seeds suffered because of cross-pollination. The lawsuit wants repayment for the damages the company has cost thousands of farmers millions of dollars, and even some innocent farmers' lives. Now, at the end of the day, the main goal of the lawsuit is to shine light on the atrocities committed by Monsanto and their co-conspirators.
Jeremy Rifkin, the president of the Foundation of Economic Trends stated that he wished the lawsuit would guide the world towards a brighter future of GM foods, and stray the world away from future corporations controlling the entire market. Rifkin also states that Monsanto and their co-conspirators represent a new exercise of influence over the agricultural industry. Later in the year, the federal government might join the case, according to Rifkin.
Monsanto, however, states that this is another attempt to take down their company and their work for the betterment of the environment. Assistant General Counsel for Monsanto is sure this case will be dismissed. The Foundation on Economic Trends and the National Family Farm Coalition launched a public education campaign to follow alongside the case. Three hundred non-government officials have consented to be legal advisors for the litigation.
The president of the National Family Farms Coalition says that farmers around the world have struggled because Genetically Modified Organisms, also known as GMOs, have not worked as advertised. Monsanto has taken the world by storm with crops consumers do not want to eat. Monsanto and their co-conspirators had a monopoly on the world's food and this litigation exposed them for all they were.

Monsanto fired back at the litigation in a prepared statement, stating they are not at fault for any issues with GMO seeds and they did not have a monopoly over GMO corn and soybean seeds. They attempted to back this statement up by saying farmers have more choices in the seed market than ever before, and that there were hundreds of suppliers in the United States alone.
All the while, their GMO seeds are advertised to have undergone intense scrutiny by regulatory agencies including the US Department of Agriculture, EPA and the FDA. Monsanto always has the farmer in mind when it comes to growing more food on less land, according to Snively. In 1998 alone, the soybean market in the United States was an astonishing 17.7 billion USD with the US corn market being slightly higher at 20 billion USD.
